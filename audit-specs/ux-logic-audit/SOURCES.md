# UX Logic Audit — Fonti e riferimenti

Le regole dell’UX Logic Audit sono allineate alle seguenti fonti. Citazione dal COMTRA_UX_Logic_Audit_Ruleset_v1.pdf, Section 10.

---

## Heuristic evaluation e usabilità

- **Nielsen, J. (1994).** “Enhancing the explanatory power of usability heuristics.” Proc. ACM CHI’94.
- **Nielsen, J. & Molich, R. (1990).** “Heuristic evaluation of user interfaces.” Proc. ACM CHI’90.
- **Nielsen Norman Group (2020–2025).** Updated heuristic definitions. nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/
- **Gerhardt-Powals, J. (1996).** Cognitive engineering principles for enhancing human-computer performance.

---

## Form UX, checkout, mobile

- **Baymard Institute (2024–2025).** 200,000+ hours UX research, 650+ guidelines, 4,400+ test sessions. baymard.com/research
- **Friedman, V. / Smashing Magazine (2022).** “A Complete Guide to Live Validation UX.” Reward-early-punish-late patterns.
- **IBM Carbon Design System (2025).** Forms pattern. carbondesignsystem.com/patterns/forms-pattern/
- **GOV.UK Design System (2025).** One-thing-per-page pattern, task-list pattern for complex journeys.

---

## Design system e component states

- **Google Material Design 3 (2025).** Navigation patterns, component states, responsive layout.
- **Apple Human Interface Guidelines (2025).** Component state requirements, navigation patterns.

---

## Dark patterns e etica

- **Nielsen Norman Group (2024).** “Deceptive Patterns in UX.” nngroup.com/articles/deceptive-patterns/
- **Brignull, H. (2023).** “Deceptive Patterns: exposing the tricks tech companies use.” deceptive.design
- **Princeton / U. Chicago (2019).** Dark patterns study: 10% of 11,000 e-commerce sites use deceptive patterns.
- **University of Zurich (2019).** 95% of 240 free trending Google Play apps contain dark patterns.

Normativa: EU DSA, California CPRA, FTC guidelines su dark pattern e consenso.

---

## Data tables e layout

- **Industry article (2025).** “Data Table Design UX Patterns & Best Practices.”
- **Coyle, A. / UX Planet (2025).** “Best Practices for Usable and Efficient Data Tables.”
- **Nielsen Norman Group (2024).** “Breakpoints in Responsive Design.”

---

## Error handling e empty states

- **UXPin research:** ogni errore deve comunicare What, Why, How to fix.
- **Clearleft (2023).** “Eliminating Dead Ends for a Better User Journey.”

---

## Internationalization

- **Lokalise / Crowdin / W3C (2025–2026).** Internationalization guidelines: text expansion, RTL, string externalization.

---

## Mappatura regole → fonti (esempi)

- **C1 System Feedback:** Nielsen H1, Material Design states.
- **C3 Form UX:** Baymard, Carbon Forms, Smashing Magazine live validation.
- **C5 Content & Copy:** Nielsen H2, H8.
- **C6 Error / Empty:** UXPin, NNGroup H9.
- **C7 Data Tables:** industry references and UX pattern articles.
- **C8 Responsive:** NNGroup Breakpoints, Material.
- **C10 Dark Patterns:** NNGroup Deceptive Patterns, Brignull, studi Princeton/Zurich.
- **C11 i18n:** W3C, Lokalise/Crowdin, GOV.UK.

Per estendere le regole, preferire riferimenti a queste fonti e documentare il link in questo file.
